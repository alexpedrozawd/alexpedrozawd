Deck: Simic Merfolk (UG)

Fill the battlefield with merfolk and grow them to victory

The Ixalan merfolk are a tribe with plenty of variations, Full Aggro (Stomp or Tokens), MidRange (+ Stamina for LateGame), Control (The least viable mode comparing with other decks in MetaControl). Particularly i prefer Aggro without tokens, where i use the merfolk themselves to buff. And that's the idea even in MODERN today, i found it interesting and decided to try to fit a similar strategy.

We no longer have the islandwalk that's used in modern, but to make them unlockable we use cards like Herald of Secret Streams, combining with Metallic Mimic, Deeproot Elite and other possible but not listed in my deck after RIX, being some of them Vineshaper Mystic, Deeproot Champion, Jade Bearer, River Herald's Boon, Shapers of Nature among others.

Due to its UG color combination it does not become so difficult to let your deck quick to hit and "smart" to defend itself. Knowing how to merge the magic between offensives and defenses becomes an interesting deck at least. Knowing how to mount/use your side you get almost without a blind spot, after all we have blue and his famous "CANCEL", counter better than this!? Green and Blue also have interesting cards that can help your creatures survive, abilities such as hexproof, + X/+X, return to hand, etc. In addition to this, the newts make it easier to hit without being worry about blocking, because cards like @River Sneak, @Mist-Cloaked Herald, buffed with Metallic Mimic, Deeproot Elite will make your opponent think of why they did not remove while they were only 1/1.

Again, depending on the idea you may have a Goblins-style deck in its prime when either we killed on Turn 4 or died on Turn 5. Because being a creature-needing deck constantly beating, my idea was putting as many creatures as possible, but not forgetting the magic to have answers and support when necessary. But you need to have more than 22 creatures on the deck if you think about being fast, otherwise your deck will slow down and do not scale well LateGame, unless you can leave these as long as possible alive. But if you save mana for the answer, you do not low creature, keep in play an amount of creatures X+1, where X is the number of creatures in your opponent's field.

I wanted to leave my deck list, but better not! Do not forget cards like Kopala, Warden of Waves, Kumena, Tyrant of Orazca and Merfolk Mistbinder that do not even need to be mentioned, such as its grandeur and obligatoriness in the current STANDARD for this tribal of glamorous mermaids.

Daniel Alves
