Deck: Gruul Dinosaurs (GR)

The most powerfull dinosaurs deck until this moment on Standard

Rivals of Ixalan was released and bring news cards to dinosaurs tribe. Unfortunately, despite the great variety o cards, almost none of them together brings a competitive sinergy to play against the big one decks.

But don't be sad child, we can do a good deck to play against everyone and still have fun! Let's take a look.

Those cards are our mana base and ramp to play big dinosaurs as soon as possible. Otepec Huntmaster decreases de mana cost of dinosaurs, and gave them haste, it's a powerfull ability and must be played as soon as we can. Drover of the Mighty don't gives haste, but they fix our mana base if you have to play red cards, since our mana base it's almost entirely green.

Commune with Dinosaurs it's a great sorcery, because like the Drover, they can fix mana base too, and if you don't need mana, you can look at the five cards on the top of your library and get a good dino, and this sorcery cost's only one forest. Thunderherd Migration is one of the best cards that Rivals of Ixalan bring to this tribe, a 1G sorcery that puts a basic land on play tapped makes a very good ramp. It's almost certain that you will have four lands on battlefield on turn three, if used on turn two.

Savage Stomp it's our only and best removal card. This is not a instant spell, but works perfectly with Deathgorge Scavenger and Ripjaw Raptor.

Samut, the Tested is the best choice of all planeswalkers on standard. You can give double strike to your dinos, do 2 of damage divided by your choose to any target/players, include our Ripjaw Raptor to draw cards or remove a opponent creature. The ultimate ability it's a finisher, really strong and we can put two of any dinosaurs in our deck on battlefield.

Now with this cards we can begin to play. All of them can enter on battlefield sooner, because at this point you have used one or more of ramp cards on the first two turns.

Deathgorge Scavanger it's a very agressive card, you can make them 4/3 as they enter on battlefield exiling a creature card from a graveyard, put a +1/+1 counter and make it fight against other creature with Savage Stomp, and give haste with Otepec Huntmaster. It's not over yet: when it attacks, give more +1/+1 with creature that him ate on this turn. A 6/4 with haste on turn three.

Ripjaw Raptor can enter on turn three with haste, a 4/5 on this step and everytime they suffer damage, you draw a card. It's a strong one, all dinosaurs deck must have them.

Regisaur Alpha? A 4/4 dinosaurs with a companion 3/3 with trample (in total: 7 power), and giving haste to all others dinosaurs. Just to remember: you can have a Otepec to give haste to this creature.

Those are the big finishers of our deck. When any of them enter the battlefield, our opponents visibly despair.

Etali, Primal Storm is a gamble card. When you attack with it, you can play something for free or not, if you have bad luck and exile a land. But only the fact of exiling a card from the top of your and your opponent library, you can use they without pay cost of mana, is something powerful. Plus, it's a 6/6.

The most powerful/priority deckn is the Ghalta. If you already have an Regissaur Alpha on the battlefield, you can probably play Ghalta with haste on turn 5! A 12/12 with trample on turn five it's a nightmare for opponents if they are not countered and don't have removal spells. In addition, the planewsalker Samut, the Tested can give double strike to him. End of the game.

Carnage Tyrant it's INSANE. A 7/6 body with trample, can't be countered and have hexproof, it's INSANE. It's absolutely the best finisher of the deck. Why? Because they can be countered and removed, against control decks it's a must have.

Hope you guys like this deck, of all those we have tested, this is the one that got the best result against competitive decks.

Have a nice day!
